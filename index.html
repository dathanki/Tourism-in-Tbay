Centennial Park

One of the City's biggest and finest trails, picnic spots and bike trails. Take a look at Thunder Bay parks for some more info about Parks in the Bay.

Beauty runs down the North

Kakabeka Falls, a treasure trove waiting to be explored, located just west of the City. Ontario provides a great resource for more on Kakabeka Falls

Exploring a different world

Shipwrecks, coral reefs, marine life and a whole new world to explore. We recommend paying Thunder Bay Country Diving a visit before going in for a splash.

Calm waters to take in the moment

Thunder Bay is home to Lake Superior, one of the largest lakes in the continent, home to the sleeping Giant and great for Kayaking! The folks at Superior Paddling will get you on the waters in no time.

A taste of Northern Brews

After a long day's full of activities what better to do than sit back and drink to the Sleeping Giant? Visit The Sleeping Giant Brewing Company for some tasty brews.

The Sleeping Giant

The Sleeping Giant is one of Thunder Bay's most valued attractions, a beautiful scene and park in Lake Superior waiting to be experienced. Visit The Sleeping Giant for some great info on the Adventures that await.

History modernised

Thunder Bay's Historical Museum features content from the past, present and the future, they're keen on making sure the community enjoy equal opportunity to participate in public life, culture, and an exploration of our collective past. Thunder Bay Historical Museum for swanky history lesson.

A Flashback in Time

Fort William Historical Park is a great place to take a moment and step into a time machine to a place where the Blackfoot stockpiled fur to trade with the English. Visit Fort William Historical Park for a sweet deal on Time travel to the 1800s.
